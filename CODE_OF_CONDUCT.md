# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Sprout exists to serve children and families in genuinely difficult situations. Every person who contributes to this project — developer, clinician, AAC user, parent, or translator — deserves to be treated with respect.

We are committed to making participation in this project a welcoming experience for everyone, regardless of age, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, level of experience, nationality, race, religion, or sexual orientation.

## Expected Behaviour

- Be kind and patient, especially with people new to AAC, open source, or both.
- Assume good intent. Most people contributing to an AAC project are motivated by genuine care.
- Give and receive feedback graciously. Clinical and design feedback in particular can feel personal — keep it focused on the work.
- Respect that families and AAC users have authority on their own lived experience. Developers and clinicians do not override that.

## Unacceptable Behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind.
- Dismissing or talking over AAC users, families, or people with disabilities.
- Publishing others' private information without consent.
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement

Violations can be reported to [hello@sproutaac.org](mailto:hello@sproutaac.org). All reports are handled confidentially. Maintainers will respond within 72 hours.

Anyone asked to stop unacceptable behaviour is expected to comply immediately. Violations may result in temporary or permanent removal from the project.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, Discord, and any other forum where community members represent the project.
